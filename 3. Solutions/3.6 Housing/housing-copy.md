### SECTION 3 — THE CHALLENGE

**Heading:** Three AC Problems That Cost Housing Developers Money

**Tenant Complaints About Comfort and Noise**
In a residential development, AC performance directly affects tenant satisfaction and lease renewal. A fixed-speed compressor cycles on and off at full load — the noise spike wakes light sleepers and the temperature swings between too hot and too cold create persistent discomfort. The inverter compressor in iCLIMA AIR split AC modulates output continuously: once the apartment reaches the thermostat set point, the compressor slows to maintain it — no cycling, no noise spike. Comfortable humidity is maintained between 40%–60%, avoiding the dry-air discomfort that ordinary AC produces even when the temperature reading is correct.

**Energy Bills That Affect Tenant Retention**
Residential tenants compare energy costs when renewing or choosing a property. An apartment fitted with SEER A+++ inverter AC uses 10%–20% less energy than an apartment with an ordinary inverter unit running the same hours. The difference in the monthly electricity bill becomes a retention argument for the developer and a switching cost for the tenant. At scale — 200 apartments across a single development — that energy differential is a measurable asset in the property specification, not a marginal benefit.

**Per-Unit Cost Consistency Across a Large Residential Build**
A 500-apartment development needs consistent AC specification across every unit — same BTU, same refrigerant charge, same air handler dimensions, same certifications, same documentation — without a spec change or price increase mid-project that forces procurement renegotiation and delays handover. iCLIMA AIR's 3,000,000 units per year production capacity absorbs your project volume into scheduled runs. 30-day lead time from order confirmation. Low MOQ per SKU means a developer can specify different BTU sizes across floor plan types without minimum volume penalties on any individual size.
