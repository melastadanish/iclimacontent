## SECTION 4 — THE PRODUCTION SYSTEM

### Automated from Pipe to Packing

Manual production steps introduce variation. Variation is where defects originate. The iCLIMA AIR production system removes the manual step from every critical process and replaces it with an automated equivalent that produces a consistent output regardless of operator or shift.

**Automatic pipe assembly welding**
Pipe joints are assembled and welded by machine. Manual welding produces joints that vary in penetration depth and joint temperature based on operator technique. Automatic welding eliminates that variable entirely. Every joint meets the same specification on unit one and unit three million.

**Automatic compressor assembly**
Compressor fitting is controlled to specified torque values by the assembly system. Under-tightening produces leaks. Over-tightening damages the compressor housing. Automatic assembly hits the specification on every unit without depending on an operator's calibration of force.

**Automatic welding**
Welding penetration depth is machine-controlled, not operator-dependent. Shallow penetration produces weak joints that fail in field conditions. Deep penetration damages surrounding components. The automated system holds depth to specification across the full production run.

**Automatic vacuum leak detection**
This is the non-negotiable step. Every finished unit is tested under vacuum before it is packed. The vacuum test identifies refrigerant leaks that are not visible to the human eye — leaks that would only appear after the unit was installed and charged in the field. 100% coverage means zero units leave the facility with an undetected leak. A sampling approach — testing one unit in fifty, for example — would pass approximately 49 units for every leak it catches at a 2% occurrence rate. iCLIMA AIR tests every unit.

**Automatic packing**
Damage during packing is the last point at which a tested unit can be compromised before shipment. Automatic packing removes the handling variation of manual packing at the final stage. A unit that passed vacuum testing arrives at the buyer's warehouse in the condition it was packed.

**High speed punching**
Fin geometry determines heat exchange efficiency. Fins produced by high speed punching hold their geometry within specification across the full production run. Variation in fin geometry affects the unit's rated BTU performance — punching precision at volume is not a cosmetic consideration.

**Visualized, digitized, and traceable production system**
Every unit's production history is recorded and traceable through the system. The traceability record covers each production stage. If a quality issue is identified at any point, the system identifies which units passed through the same stage under the same conditions. This is how a 73% reduction in PPM for missing parts and incorrect assembly was achieved — the system identifies the pattern, not just the individual failure.

## SECTION 6 — ISO 9001

### The Quality Management System Behind the Numbers

ISO 9001:2015 is not a product certificate. It is a third-party audited certification of the management system that governs how the factory operates — how processes are defined, how non-conformances are recorded, how corrective actions are implemented, and how quality performance is reviewed.

For a buyer placing an order 30 days out, ISO 9001 answers a specific question: will the unit I receive on day 30 be the same as the sample I approved? An ISO 9001 system requires documented procedures for every production step, records of every non-conformance, and regular management reviews of quality performance. It cannot guarantee zero defects — no system can. What it does guarantee is that the factory has a documented process for finding, recording, and correcting the source of every defect, not just the individual unit.

The iCLIMA AIR facility is ISO 9001 certified by a third-party accredited certification body. The certificate is available for review at inquiry.

## SECTION 7 — PRE-SHIPMENT PROCESS

### What Happens Before a Container Leaves

A completed production run goes through a defined pre-shipment sequence before loading.

**Vacuum leak detection:** Already completed on every unit during production. No re-testing is required at this stage — the record from the production-stage test is part of the unit's traceability file.

**Packing inspection:** Completed units are inspected for packing integrity before container loading. Physical damage in transit originates from packing failures, not production failures. This step closes that gap.

**Documentation check:** Certification documents, test reports, Declarations of Conformity, and packing lists are verified against the order before the shipment is sealed. No missing documents are discovered at the destination port.

**Test report inclusion:** The relevant test reports and certification documents are included in the physical shipment package. They arrive with the product — not separately by email, not at buyer request after arrival.

## SECTION 11 — FAQ

**What is the defect rate?**
Below 0.1%. This is the defect rate across 3,000,000 units per year. The figure reflects the output of an automated production system with 15 years of operation, not a target or an estimate.

**How is vacuum leak detection carried out?**
Every finished unit is tested individually under vacuum before packing. The test identifies refrigerant leaks that are invisible to visual inspection. Because the test is automated and applied to 100% of units, no unit leaves the facility without a passed test record. Batch testing or sampling would miss leaks that fall between tested units — iCLIMA AIR does not use sampling for leak detection.

**What does ISO 9001 certification cover?**
ISO 9001 certifies the manufacturing facility's quality management system — the documented procedures, non-conformance records, corrective action processes, and management reviews that govern how every unit is produced. It is third-party audited. The certificate is available at inquiry.

**What documentation ships with every order?**
Each shipment includes: certification documents applicable to the destination market, test reports, Declaration of Conformity (for CE-covered markets), packing lists, and traceability records. Documentation is confirmed at the quotation stage and verified against the order before shipping.

**What happens if a unit fails in the field?**
Every unit produced in the iCLIMA AIR facility is traceable through the production system. If a unit fails in the field, the production record identifies which stage it passed through, under what conditions, and which other units were produced in the same batch. This traceability is the corrective action mechanism behind the 73% reduction in missing part and incorrect assembly PPM.

**How does quality hold at 3,000,000 units per year?**
Volume does not reduce quality when the quality system is automated rather than operator-dependent. The automated production steps — pipe welding, compressor assembly, welding, leak detection, packing — produce the same output on unit three million as on unit one. The visualized, digitized, traceable production system monitors the output continuously. The 20% first-pass rate improvement and 73% PPM reduction are the measurable outcomes of maintaining an automated system at scale.
