## SECTION 3 — THE NORTH AMERICAN MARKET

### The North American AC Market — What Importers Need to Know

North America operates on the DOE's SEER2 standard, which replaced the older SEER rating in 2023. The minimum efficiency threshold for residential split AC is 15 SEER2 (Southeast) and 14.3 SEER2 (North). iCLIMA AIR inverter split AC is rated at 25 SEER2 — well above the minimum, positioned at the premium efficiency tier that the inverter AC segment commands. AHRI certification is the performance standard that HVAC contractors and distributors require before specifying a unit; UL safety certification is mandatory for US and Canadian market entry.

The North American market is cooling-dominant in the South and West, with growing heat pump adoption replacing gas furnaces in the Northeast under federal efficiency incentives. iCLIMA AIR's inverter heat pump variant — cooling and heating in one unit — serves both demand profiles from the same SKU. Window AC at 9,000–24,000 BTU covers the rental and retrofit segment where split installation is not viable.

## SECTION 4 — CERTIFICATIONS FOR NORTH AMERICA

### Certifications Required to Import AC into North America

HVAC products entering the US market without UL safety certification face CBP detainment and CPSC market action. AHRI certification is required for any unit to be listed in contractor specification software and utility rebate programmes — without it, the product is invisible to the professional HVAC channel. iCLIMA AIR holds both before any container ships.

| Certification | Issuing Body | Coverage | Why North American Importers Need It |
|---|---|---|---|
| UL | UL Solutions (formerly Underwriters Laboratories) | US and Canada | Safety certification — required for US market entry and Canadian CSA equivalence |
| AHRI | Air-Conditioning, Heating, and Refrigeration Institute | North America | Performance standard — required for contractor specification and utility rebate programmes |
| CB | IECEE CB Scheme | Global baseline | IEC safety baseline — supports UL testing, accepted by US notified bodies |

DOE SEER2 rating is a product efficiency specification, not a separate certification body. iCLIMA AIR inverter split AC is rated at 25 SEER2 — this figure appears on the EnergyGuide label that ships with every North American unit.

## SECTION 10 — FAQ

**Q: What certifications are required to import AC into the United States?**
UL safety certification is required for US market entry — without it, the product cannot be legally sold or installed under the National Electrical Code. AHRI performance certification is required for listing in the ACCA Manual S specification database and for eligibility in utility rebate programmes that drive contractor purchasing decisions. CB certification provides the IEC baseline that supports UL testing. iCLIMA AIR holds all three before shipment, so importers clear US Customs with a complete documentation package.

**Q: What is the SEER2 rating of iCLIMA AIR products?**
iCLIMA AIR inverter split AC is rated at 25 SEER2 under the DOE 2023 standard — above the 15 SEER2 Southeast minimum and 14.3 SEER2 North minimum. The EnergyGuide label documenting this rating ships with every North American unit. EER performance is also measured to AHRI 210/240 — the standard used by US contractors for cooling capacity verification. Higher SEER2 ratings increase eligibility for federal and utility efficiency incentives that reduce the cost to the end buyer.

**Q: What voltage specification does iCLIMA AIR use for North America?**
North American models are manufactured at 208–230V/60Hz — the standard residential and light commercial voltage in the US and Canada. This is factory-set, not a field adaptation. European 220V/50Hz models are separate SKUs. Confirm your destination market at inquiry and the quotation specifies the correct electrical variant.

**Q: What is the lead time from China to North America?**
Production lead time is 30 days from order confirmation. Stock items ship in 10 days. East Coast ports add 5–7 days. iCLIMA AIR provides full export documentation; the buyer arranges freight or nominates their forwarder.

**Q: Can I import AC under my own brand into the US market?**

The SEER2 rating, EER performance, and 208–230V/60Hz specification remain unchanged. The EnergyGuide label data is re-filed under the buyer's brand. Confirm your product type and US destination state at inquiry — DOE regional efficiency minimums vary by climate zone.

**Q: How does factory-direct compare to North American wholesale distributors?**
