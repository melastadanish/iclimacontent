## SECTION 3 — THE AFRICAN MARKET

### The African AC Market — What Importers Need to Know

Africa's AC market is cooling-dominant across all sub-regions. Ambient temperatures regularly exceed 40°C in West Africa, 45°C in the Sahel, and 50°C in parts of North Africa — conditions that cause underrated AC units to trip out, underperform, or fail early. T3 rating (ambient to 54°C, operation to 68°C) is the product specification standard that separates high-ambient AC from standard European or North American products. iCLIMA AIR's inverter split AC maintains full BTU output at 50°C ambient, with no capacity derating.

Power infrastructure varies by country. Many African markets have unreliable grid supply and high generator use. iCLIMA AIR's Gen Mode allows the inverter split AC to run at 30%, 50%, or 70% of rated power — generator-compatible without requiring an oversized generator to start the unit. This is a commercially significant feature for distributors selling into markets where generator use is standard.

CB certification is the import baseline accepted across most African customs authorities. CE is also held — useful for distributors selling into markets with European import preference or for buyers supplying francophone African markets with EU-aligned standards.

## SECTION 4 — CERTIFICATIONS FOR AFRICA

### Certifications for the African Market

African import regulations vary by country, but CB certification is the widely accepted safety baseline across the continent. Markets with strong European trade ties — North Africa, West Africa francophone countries, East Africa — also accept CE. Neither certification requires re-testing upon arrival when the original certificate and test report are presented at customs.

| Certification | Issuing Body | Coverage | Why African Importers Need It |
|---|---|---|---|
| CB | IECEE CB Scheme | Global baseline | Accepted at customs across African markets — most common import safety baseline |
| CE | European Commission — self-declaration | EU + widely accepted in francophone Africa and North Africa | Accepted in markets with EU trade preference · covers North Africa imports |
| ISO 9001 | Bureau Veritas / TÜV | Quality management | Required by government procurement tenders and large distributor RFQs |

## SECTION 10 — FAQ

**Q: What certifications are needed to import AC into Africa?**
CB (IEC safety baseline) is the most widely accepted certification across African customs authorities. CE is accepted in North African markets and francophone West and Central African countries with EU trade preference agreements. ISO 9001 is required by government procurement tenders in countries including Kenya, Nigeria, and Ghana. iCLIMA AIR holds CB, CE, and ISO 9001 — all three ship with the container. Confirm your destination country at inquiry for market-specific documentation requirements.

**Q: What does T3 rating mean for African markets?**
T3 is the IEC climate class for tropical high-ambient conditions. A T3-rated AC maintains full rated cooling capacity at 54°C ambient and continues to operate at 68°C ambient — the conditions found during peak season in West Africa, the Sahel, and the Arabian Peninsula. iCLIMA AIR's inverter split AC is T3-rated: 9,000–24,000 BTU output at 50°C ambient is the same as at 25°C. Non-T3-rated units derate by 20%–40% in high-ambient conditions, meaning the buyer pays for 18,000 BTU and receives 11,000 BTU when it is needed most.

**Q: How does Gen Mode work for generator markets?**
Gen Mode allows the inverter split AC to operate at 30%, 50%, or 70% of rated power draw — selectable from the remote control. A standard 18,000 BTU unit draws approximately 1,800W at full load. In Gen Mode at 50% power, the unit draws approximately 900W, making it compatible with a 2.5 kVA generator rather than requiring a 5 kVA unit. This is a factory feature — not a field modification — available on iCLIMA AIR inverter split AC models.

**Q: What is the voltage specification for Africa?**
iCLIMA AIR inverter split AC for African markets is manufactured at 220V/50Hz — the standard grid voltage across most of sub-Saharan Africa, West Africa, East Africa, and North Africa. South Africa uses 230V/50Hz — compatible with 220V units within the operating tolerance range. Confirm your destination country at inquiry for voltage verification.

**Q: What is the lead time from China to African ports?**

East African ports (Mombasa, Dar es Salaam) take 18–25 days. North African ports (Casablanca, Alexandria) take 18–22 days. iCLIMA AIR provides full export documentation; the buyer arranges freight.
