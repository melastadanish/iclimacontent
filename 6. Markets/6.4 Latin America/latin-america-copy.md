## SECTION 3 — THE LATIN AMERICAN MARKET

### The Latin American AC Market — What Importers Need to Know

Latin America is a cooling-dominant market driven by tropical and subtropical climate zones across Central America, the Caribbean, Colombia, and coastal South America. Ambient temperatures regularly exceed 35°C in urban centres, with humidity levels that raise the effective cooling load significantly. The market requires inverter split AC for residential and commercial installations, with commercial cassette and duct AC for retail and hospitality.

Regulatory fragmentation is the defining challenge for Latin American importers. Colombia requires RETIQ energy efficiency certification issued by ONAC-accredited laboratories — without it, the product cannot be sold on the Colombian market. Central American markets (Honduras, El Salvador, Guatemala, Nicaragua, Costa Rica) require RTCA (Reglamento Técnico Centroamericano) compliance. The Caribbean and other South American markets accept CB as a baseline. iCLIMA AIR holds RETIQ, RTCA, and CB — covering the certification landscape from Mexico to Argentina without requiring the importer to file separately in each country.

The 60Hz electrical standard across Latin America and the Caribbean means European 50Hz-rated units are not directly compatible. iCLIMA AIR's Latin American configuration is factory-set at 220V/60Hz.

## SECTION 10 — FAQ

**Q: What certifications are required to import AC into Colombia?**
RETIQ (Reglamento Técnico de Etiquetado de Eficiencia Energética) is mandatory for air conditioners sold in Colombia. It is issued by laboratories accredited by ONAC (Organismo Nacional de Acreditación de Colombia) and is required for customs clearance. Products without RETIQ certification are withdrawn from the Colombian market by the Superintendencia de Industria y Comercio. CB certification is also accepted as a safety baseline. iCLIMA AIR holds both — the RETIQ certificate ships with every Colombian-bound container.

**Q: What is RTCA certification and which markets require it?**
RTCA (Reglamento Técnico Centroamericano) is the technical regulation governing product safety and efficiency standards for the Central American Common Market — Honduras, El Salvador, Guatemala, Nicaragua, and Costa Rica. RTCA compliance is mandatory for customs clearance at any Central American border. iCLIMA AIR holds RTCA certification, covering the full Central American market from a single certificate. Puerto Rico, as a US territory, requires UL and AHRI — confirm your destination at inquiry.

**Q: What electrical specification do Latin American units use?**
iCLIMA AIR Latin American models are factory-set at 220V/60Hz — the standard residential voltage across Colombia, Central America, the Caribbean, and most of South America. Brazil uses 127V/60Hz in some states and 220V/60Hz in others — confirm the Brazilian destination city at inquiry. European 220V/50Hz variants are separate SKUs and are not compatible with Latin American 60Hz grids without frequency conversion.

**Q: What products are most popular in the Latin American market?**
Inverter split AC (9,000–24,000 BTU) is the core residential and light commercial product across Colombia, Central America, and the Caribbean. Commercial cassette and duct AC (12,000–60,000 BTU) is specified for hotels, offices, and retail in major cities. Window AC serves the Caribbean island market and dense urban apartment segments. All are available in RETIQ, RTCA, and CB certified versions at 220V/60Hz.
