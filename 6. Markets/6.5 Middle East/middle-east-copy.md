## SECTION 3 — THE MIDDLE EAST MARKET

### The Gulf AC Market — What Importers Need to Know

The Middle East and Gulf region is one of the world's most demanding AC markets. Peak summer ambient temperatures in the UAE, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, and Qatar regularly reach 48°C–52°C outdoors; rooftop condensers in direct solar exposure can see surface temperatures above 60°C. AC units that are not T3-rated for these conditions derate significantly — an 18,000 BTU unit may deliver 11,000–13,000 BTU at 50°C ambient, leaving end users complaining about product failure when the product is simply the wrong specification.

Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) markets require GMRK (Gulf Mark) certification for all imported electrical and electronic products, including air conditioners. GMRK is administered by the GSO (Gulf Standardisation Organisation) and is mandatory for customs clearance across all six GCC member states. Products entering the UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, or Oman without GMRK face port confiscation and return-to-origin at the importer's cost.

The Gulf market also has a growing residential and commercial construction pipeline — hospitality, residential towers, offices, and retail — driving demand for multi-split and commercial AC alongside the core residential wall-mount split.

## SECTION 10 — FAQ

**Q: What certifications are required to import AC into the Gulf (GCC)?**
GMRK (Gulf Mark) certification is mandatory for air conditioners entering any GCC member state — UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, and Oman. It is administered by the Gulf Standardisation Organisation (GSO) and is required for customs clearance at all GCC ports of entry. Products without GMRK are confiscated and returned at the importer's cost. CB certification provides the IEC safety baseline that supports GMRK testing. iCLIMA AIR holds both — documentation ships with every Gulf-bound container.

**Q: What does T3 rating mean in the Gulf context?**
T3 is the IEC climate class for tropical and high-ambient operation. A T3-rated air conditioner maintains its full rated BTU capacity at 54°C ambient and continues to operate at 68°C ambient — the conditions found during peak summer in the UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Kuwait. Standard (T1) AC units are rated at 43°C ambient. At 52°C ambient, a non-T3-rated unit may deliver 60%–70% of its rated BTU output. iCLIMA AIR's inverter split AC is T3-rated across all BTU sizes from 9,000 to 24,000 BTU.

**Q: Does iCLIMA AIR hold SASO certification for Saudi Arabia?**
SASO (Saudi Standards, Metrology and Quality Organisation) certification is held at factory level. Confirm at inquiry whether your destination is Saudi Arabia — SASO Mark requirements for imported AC vary by product category and year of manufacture. iCLIMA AIR will confirm the applicable Saudi certification documentation at the quotation stage.

**Q: What refrigerant is used — is it approved for Gulf markets?**
iCLIMA AIR split AC uses R32 refrigerant — GWP 675, fully compliant with GCC chemical import regulations. R32 is accepted by GMRK and does not face the import restrictions applied to higher-GWP refrigerants in the EU. Window AC uses R290. Neither product uses R410a. R32 pre-charge is factory-set; no field refrigerant handling is required for installation.

**Q: What is the lead time from China to Gulf ports?**

Dammam (Saudi Arabia) takes 18–25 days. Doha (Qatar) takes 20–25 days. iCLIMA AIR provides full export documentation; the buyer arranges freight or nominates their freight forwarder.

Gulf regional AC distributors add two to three margin layers between the Chinese factory and the Gulf importer. iCLIMA AIR ships factory-direct — GMRK certified, T3-rated, from a 30,000 sqm factory running 3,000,000 units per year.
