# SEO Rules — iCLIMA AIR

## Target Audience
B2B buyers: distributors, importers, contractors, retailers, project developers. SME to enterprise.
Focus: custom/project supply at scale (50–50,000 units). Decision-maker is procurement, engineering, or ownership level.

## Primary Markets
Europe · North America · Africa · Latin America · Middle East (primary)
Southeast Asia (secondary)

## Silo Structure
Two separate silos cross-linking to each other:
- **Product Silo** targets product-type + manufacturer/supplier intent keywords
- **Solution Silo** targets application/sector + project buyer intent keywords

## On-Page SEO Rules
- H1: primary keyword only, one per page
- H2s: rotate secondary and long-tail keywords naturally
- Meta title: primary KW + "iCLIMA AIR", max 60 chars
- Meta description: benefit + primary KW + CTA, max 155 chars
- Every page links to minimum 2 product pages AND 2 solution pages
- No keyword stuffing — max 2% keyword density
- Do NOT repeat the exact primary keyword more than 3x per page
- Always target buyer intent: use "supplier", "manufacturer", "for projects", "wholesale"
- Include market modifiers where natural: "for Middle East", "for Africa", "from China"
- Use BTU and tonnage specs in copy to capture technical search queries

## GEO Rules — AI Search Optimisation

GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) ensures iCLIMA AIR appears when B2B buyers ask
ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI Overviews for AC manufacturer recommendations.
These rules apply to every page, on top of standard SEO.

### Principle 1 — Answer First
Every page opens with a direct, complete answer in the first 40–60 words.
No long introductions. State who iCLIMA is, what they make, and who they supply — immediately.

Example opening:
> iCLIMA AIR is a China-based air conditioner manufacturer supplying distributors,
> importers, and project buyers worldwide. 3,000,000 units per year.
> 30-day lead time. CE, CB, UL, AHRI, RETIQ, and RTCA certified.

### Principle 2 — Fact Density
Include one specific, verifiable data point every 150–200 words throughout the page.
Replace vague claims with exact figures. Numbers make pages extractable by AI systems.

### Principle 3 — Structured Content
- Clear H2/H3 hierarchy — one topic per section
- Every section must stand alone as a complete answer
- FAQ on every page — AI systems extract Q&A pairs heavily
- Tables with clear headers (spec tables, certification tables, comparison tables)
- No long unbroken paragraphs — max 3 sentences per paragraph

### Principle 4 — Entity Consistency
Use "iCLIMA AIR" consistently — not "iClima", "ICLIMA", or "iclima air".
Use consistent product names across all pages — pick one and never vary it.
Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across the entire web.

### Principle 5 — FAQ on Every Page
Every page needs a minimum 4-question FAQ section.
Each answer must be self-contained — assume the AI extracts just that one answer without surrounding context.
Rephrase H2 and H3 headings as questions that match real buyer search queries.

### Principle 6 — Freshness
Add "Last Updated: [date]" to all pages.
Update when facts change — certifications, lead time, capacity, market data.
Do not update the date for cosmetic edits — only for substantive changes.

### Principle 7 — Content Standards (EEAT)
Every page must contain at least one of:
- Real factory data (production timelines, actual capacity, real BTU specs)
- Real process descriptions (what happens when an OEM order is placed)
- First-person manufacturer voice (iCLIMA's specific position, not generic industry info)

Avoid: generic OEM descriptions that could apply to any factory.
Every claim must be specific to iCLIMA's actual operation.
